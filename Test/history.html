Brief History

Ancient Cryptography

Egypt (1900 BC): Early forms of cryptography appeared in Egypt with inscriptions in non-standard hieroglyphs used to encode messages on tombs and monuments. These were not necessarily secret codes but were intended to make the inscriptions appear more dignified.

What is Cryptography

cryp·tog·ra·phy
/kripˈtäɡrəfē/ (noun)

The discipline that embodies the principles, means, and methods for the transformation of data in order to hide their semantic content, prevent their unauthorized use, or prevent their undetected modification.

-National Institute of Standards and Technology

To put it simply, Cryptography is the process of encoding information so that only the the person who the message was for can read it.
